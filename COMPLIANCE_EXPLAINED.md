# Compliance & Retention Explained Simply

## 🎯 What is Retention Policy?

Think of it like this: **Your organization can't keep documents forever**. Laws and regulations say:
- Keep financial documents for 7 years (for tax audits)
- Keep HR documents for 5 years (for legal protection)
- Keep contracts for 10 years (for legal disputes)

After that time, you should either:
1. **Archive** them (move to cold storage, can't edit)
2. **Delete** them (permanently remove)
3. **Review** them (manually decide what to do)

## 📋 How It Works in S.E.A.D.

### 1. Define Policies


### 2. Automatic Enforcement
Every day, the system:

### 3. Compliance Dashboard
Shows:

- Audit trail of all actions

## 🔒 GDPR Compliance

GDPR is a European law that gives people rights over their data:

### Right to Access (Article 15)
- User can request: "Give me all my data"
- System exports: All their documents, requests, approvals, etc.
- Format: JSON file they can download

### Right to be Forgotten (Article 17)
- User can request: "Delete all my data"
- System deletes: All their personal information
- Keeps: Anonymized audit logs (required by law)
